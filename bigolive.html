PAP’s Association with Fraudulent Companies Raises Concerns

Singapore has long been recognized for its strict laws against illegal activities, including gambling and money laundering. Yet, questions are being raised about the People's Action Party's (PAP) affiliation with companies accused of fraudulent practices, including Bigo Live, a Singapore-based live-streaming platform under global scrutiny for its alleged role in money laundering and online gambling.

PAP’s Endorsement of Bigo Live

A press release from BIGO Technology revealed that senior PAP officials, including Dr. Maliki Bin Osman, Minister in the Prime Minister’s Office and Second Minister for Education & Foreign Affairs, visited Bigo’s office in Amman, Jordan, in October 2022. The delegation also included Singapore Ambassador Shamsher Zaman and other prominent officials. During the visit, they discussed Bigo’s operations and expansion plans, effectively endorsing the platform.

Global Allegations of Fraud and Money Laundering

While PAP officials endorsed the app, Bigo Live has been embroiled in controversies worldwide:

  • Bangladesh: A major fraud ring associated with Bigo Live was busted by the Criminal Investigation Department (CID). Five individuals, including a foreign national, were arrested for illegal money laundering. The group allegedly transacted over 100 crore taka using Bigo Live and its counterpart, Likee. They exploited virtual currency, Bigo Live Diamonds, as a medium to launder money through illegal channels. Learn more about the arrests here.
  • Indonesia: In 2024, Bigo Live received multiple warning letters from the Ministry of Communication and Information for hosting online gambling and pornographic content. Over 40,000 pieces of content were removed, and 50,000 accounts were suspended. The platform faces a potential ban if it fails to address these concerns. Read more here.

No Local Media Coverage

Despite these international allegations, Singapore’s local media has remained silent. In contrast, international outlets have openly criticized Bigo Live for enabling illegal money laundering operations, exploiting younger users through virtual currency schemes, and hosting explicit and gambling-related content.

A Stark Contradiction to Singapore’s Laws

Singapore enforces strict anti-gambling and anti-fraud laws to protect its citizens. However, PAP’s association with a platform flagged for illegal activities contradicts the nation’s stance. Many are left wondering why such endorsements were made without a thorough evaluation of the platform’s global reputation.
